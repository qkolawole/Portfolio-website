Financial Complaints

Advanced Analytics for precise financial planning

Project Overview

This Financial Complaints Analysis project aims to provide a comprehensive overview of customer complaints for a financial institution or regulatory body. The dashboard focuses on key metrics such as complaint volume, response timeliness, resolution rates, and geographic distribution. The goal is to offer insights that can improve customer service, identify areas for operational improvement, and ensure regulatory compliance.


Technical Implementation

The analysis is presented through a data visualization dashboard, built using a business intelligence tool; Tableau.The dashboard incorporates various chart types:


  • Key performance indicators (KPIs) for overall complaint metrics
  • Bar chart for year-to-date (YTD) complaint trends
  • Donut charts for timely responses and resolution rates
  • U.S. map for geographic distribution of complaints
  • Bar chart for complaints by submission type

Challenges

These are the challenges encountered during the analysis


  • Aggregating and standardizing complaint data from multiple channels (web, phone, mail, etc.)
  • Ensuring accurate and timely tracking of complaint statuses and resolutions
  • Balancing the need for detailed insights with data privacy concerns
  • Identifying trends and patterns in complaint data to drive actionable improvements
  • Meeting regulatory requirements for complaint handling and reporting

Solution

The implemented dashboard addresses these challenges by:

  • Providing clear, high-level KPIs for quick understanding of complaint status and trends
  • Visualizing geographic distribution of complaints to identify regional issues
  • Breaking down complaints by submission type to understand preferred customer channels
  • Tracking year-over-year performance to gauge improvement efforts
  • Highlighting critical metrics like timely responses and no-cost resolutions

Projected Results & Impacts

Impacts Expected

  • Improved customer satisfaction through faster complaint resolution and timely responses
  • Enhanced operational efficiency by identifying and addressing common complaint sources
  • Better resource allocation based on complaint volume and geographic distribution
  • Increased regulatory compliance through comprehensive tracking and reporting
  • Data-driven decision-making for process improvements and policy changes

Lessons Learned

Key Takeaways

  • The importance of monitoring both volume and quality metrics in complaint handling
  • The value of geographic visualization in identifying regional trends or issues
  • The significance of tracking resolution rates, particularly those resolved at no cost
  • The need for multi-channel complaint submission options to cater to diverse customer preferences
  • The potential for using complaint data to drive proactive improvements in products or services

Summary

    This Financial Complaints Analysis dashboard provides a solid foundation for data-driven customer service management and regulatory compliance. Future iterations could potentially include more advanced analytics such as sentiment analysis of complaint text, predictive models for complaint volume forecasting, or correlation studies between complaint types and specific products or services. Additionally, incorporating customer demographics or financial impact of complaints could provide a more comprehensive view of the situation and its business implications.
